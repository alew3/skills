# Style / Mood Board Design Sheet Template

Produces a single polished landscape STYLE / MOOD BOARD that LOCKS the project look (palette + lighting + grade + art style) — the look bible downstream skills thread into every generation.

**TEXT-HEAVY** (title, tile labels, hex, notes) → prefer **GPT Image 2** or **Nano Banana Pro**; aspect **16:9**. See `docs/MODEL_PROMPTING.md`.

PROJECT: [name] · ART STYLE: [photoreal | painterly concept | cel-shaded anime | flat vector | …] (not necessarily "cinematic")

LOOK BIBLE:
- Palette: [5–6 colors / hex]
- Lighting: [direction + quality, mood]
- Grade / film look: [e.g. teal-orange, desaturated, Kodak Portra, bleach bypass]
- Texture / material cues: [surfaces, grain]

LAYOUT (describe every section in the prompt):
- HEADER: "<PROJECT> — STYLE BOARD" + the art style.
- COLOR PALETTE: a strip of 5–6 labeled swatches with hex.
- MATERIAL / TEXTURE: 2–3 labeled sample tiles.
- LIGHTING / ATMOSPHERE: 1–2 labeled studies.
- MINI-VIGNETTES: 2–3 representative scenes in the chosen style, sharing the palette + lighting.
- LOOK NOTES: a short caption. ONE palette + ONE lighting logic across all tiles.

SEND VERBATIM (example filled):
```
A professional style / mood board design sheet, landscape, clean editorial infographic layout with thin rule lines and sans-serif labels. HEADER: "NEON ALIBI — STYLE BOARD", art style "neo-noir photoreal". COLOR PALETTE strip of six labeled swatches with hex: "INK #0A1A2E, TEAL #1E3ABA, AMBER #D4AF37, STEEL #7B828C, BONE #F2EFE9, BLOOD #7A1E2C". MATERIAL / TEXTURE tiles, labeled: wet asphalt, brushed metal, rain-streaked glass. LIGHTING / ATMOSPHERE studies, labeled: cool volumetric haze with warm practical rim light. MINI-VIGNETTES: three small neo-noir city scenes sharing the SAME palette and lighting. LOOK NOTES caption: "cool desaturated grade, warm practicals, 35mm shallow depth of field, subtle grain". One palette and one lighting logic across every tile, legible labels, no warped text.
```

NEGATIVE: inconsistent palette across tiles, clashing styles, unreadable labels.
